Wed 3 Jun
Lake Tekapo → Lake Pukaki → Mount Cook
~2.5 hrs total driving
❄ June: Mt Cook road (SH80) can be icy and snowy, especially in the afternoon. Check NZTA road status before heading up. Village sits at the base of Aoraki — stunning with snow on the peaks. Pack extra warm layers.
8:30 am
Optional: Mt John Summit drive ✓
📷 Mt John Summit: 360° views of Lake Tekapo, Lake Alexandrina, and the Southern Alps — the best landscape shot in the region. Toll road ~$8. Allow 20–30 mins if skies are clear. Worth doing before the petting zoo if you have time.
9:00 am
Lake Tekapo Petting Zoo ✓ Book online ahead
Hand-feed Merino sheep, alpacas, KuneKune pigs, calves. Both kids will love it. Limited spots per day — book in advance online.
48a D'Archiac Drive · Winter hours: daily 9 am–4 pm · ~45–60 mins · +64 27 900 0335
🚗 Lake Tekapo → Lake Pukaki ~45 min via SH8
📷 Lake Pukaki dam viewpoint: Pull over at the dam lookout as you approach on SH8 — your first view of the electric turquoise lake with Aoraki behind. One of the great NZ views, even more dramatic in winter with snow on the peaks. 5-minute stop, absolutely unmissable.
11:00 am
Lake Pukaki — lunch & photos ✓
Mt Cook Alpine Salmon Shop — Salmon sashimi, nigiri, hot smoked salmon. An iconic South Island food stop. Hours: daily ~9 am–5 pm · +64 3 435 0427.
Marker Bay Food Truck — Casual outdoor, fish & chips, salmon burgers. Check winter operating days as hours may be reduced in June.
📷 Marker Bay: Walk 5 mins from the carpark to the lakefront — the reflection of Aoraki in the turquoise water from this bay is one of the most photographed views in New Zealand. Best in morning light.

1.5 km return (~30 mins), flat, pram-accessible. Direct view of the Mueller Glacier terminal lake and Aoraki's face. Perfect for a cold late afternoon — less demanding than Hooker and genuinely impressive. Save Hooker for the fresh morning of Day 3.
📷 Kea Point: Mueller Glacier lake with Aoraki looming behind. In winter with snow on the valley floor, this is dramatic. Great family shot at the viewpoint platform.

Thu 4 Jun
Mount Cook → Twizel → Queenstown
Hooker Valley Track AM · ~4 hrs total driving · Checkout at breakfast
❄ June: Hooker Valley will be cold and possibly frosty — dress in multiple layers. Track may have icy patches near the glacier end. The Lindis Pass drive to Queenstown is one of NZ's most beautiful winter landscapes. Sunset ~5 pm — aim for Queenstown by 5 pm.
8:00 am
Breakfast at Old Mountaineers' · Checkout bags to car ✓
Old Mountaineers' Café — Opens 8 am. Check out, load bags, head straight to the trailhead after breakfast. It's a 5-min drive from the lodge to the Hooker Valley carpark.
8:45 am
Hooker Valley Track ✓ Aim for 2nd swing bridge
Start at the Hooker Valley carpark. Target: second suspension bridge (~5 km return, ~1.5 hrs) — this gives you the iconic Aoraki reflection in Hooker Lake on a clear day. Both suspension bridges are exciting for kids and very manageable for a 7-yr-old. The 4-yr-old will need encouragement on the way back but the bridges make it worthwhile. If energy or cold is a factor, the first bridge (2 km return, ~40 mins) is still a great experience.
📷 Hooker Valley: The suspension bridges with Aoraki behind are an iconic NZ shot. Best light is morning. Fresh snow on the valley floor in June makes this even more dramatic. Get a family shot mid-bridge with the mountain behind.
Weather backup: If the track is icy or closed, the Blue Lakes Walk (1 km, very easy) or Tasman Glacier Viewpoint (45 min return) are safe, child-friendly alternatives.
10:30 am
Back to car · Head to Twizel

SH6 via Cromwell (115 km, ~1.5 hrs) is the safer, reliable winter alternative. If Crown Range looks good, take it — it's exceptional.

📷 Crown Range summit (if taking that route): Pull over at the summit layby for a panoramic view down over the Cardrona Valley — one of the great NZ road trip shots. 5-minute stop.

Coronet Peak AM · Skyline PM
❄ June = ski season: Coronet Peak typically opens mid-June. Check coronetpeak.co.nz for the exact opening date — if open, this is a brilliant family day. Ski school runs from age 3. If snow isn't operating yet, swap the morning for Arrowtown.

Coronet Peak (if open) ✓
Beginner slopes, ski school for both kids (age 3+). Hire gear at the base. Warm, waterproof layers + sunscreen — UV is strong on snow. Allow the full morning. A half-day ski lesson for the kids is a great option.
Coronet Peak base lodge café — Hot food and warming drinks on the mountain.

Postmasters Café — Popular brunch, good food. Hours: daily ~8 am–4 pm.

Gondola up + luge runs — perfect for both ages (min 3 yrs for luge, 4-yr-old rides tandem with a parent). Allow 3–4 hrs. In winter the gondola views of The Remarkables under snow are spectacular. Do your luge runs while there's still some light (2–4 pm), then head up to dinner.
Stratosfare Restaurant (Skyline) — Buffet at the top. Kids at a lower price. Convenient dinner with views before the gondola back down.

Clay Cliffs, Omarama ✓

Our recommendation: Option A. You've already done a farm tour on Day 6 (Walter Peak) and a petting zoo on Day 2, so Willowbank is the more unique experience here — native NZ wildlife, the kiwi house, and tuatara are things you genuinely can't see elsewhere.
