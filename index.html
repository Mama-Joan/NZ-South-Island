Hand-feed Merino sheep, alpacas, KuneKune pigs, calves. Both kids will love it. Limited spots per day — book in advance online.

📷 Lake Pukaki dam viewpoint: Pull over at the dam lookout on SH8 — your first view of the electric turquoise lake with Aoraki behind. Even more dramatic in winter with snow on the peaks. Unmissable — 5-min stop.
11:00 am

1.5 km return (~30 mins), flat, pram-accessible. Direct view of Mueller Glacier and Aoraki’s face. Perfect for a cold late afternoon — less demanding than Hooker, still genuinely impressive.

Thu 4 Jun
Mount Cook → Twizel → Queenstown
Hooker Valley Track AM · ~4 hrs total driving · Checkout at breakfast
❄ June: Hooker Valley will be cold and possibly frosty — dress in multiple layers. Track may have icy patches near the glacier end. Lindis Pass drive is stunning winter tussock landscape. Sunset ~5 pm — aim for Queenstown by 5 pm.
8:00 am
Breakfast at Old Mountaineers’ · Checkout bags to car ✓
Old Mountaineers’ Café — Opens 8 am. Check out, load bags into car, head straight to the trailhead after breakfast. 5-min drive from lodge to Hooker Valley carpark.
8:45 am
Hooker Valley Track ✓ Target 2nd swing bridge
Target: second suspension bridge (~5 km return, ~1.5 hrs) — iconic Aoraki reflection in Hooker Lake on a clear day. Both suspension bridges are exciting for kids. If energy or cold is a factor, the first bridge (2 km return, ~40 mins) is still a great experience.
📷 Hooker Valley: Suspension bridges with Aoraki behind — an iconic NZ shot. Best light is morning. Fresh snow on the valley floor in June makes this even more dramatic. Get a family shot mid-bridge with the mountain behind.
Weather backup: If the track is icy or closed, the Blue Lakes Walk (1 km) or Tasman Glacier Viewpoint (45 min return) are safe, child-friendly alternatives.
10:30 am

Queenstown → Wanaka Day Trip
~1–1.5 hr each way

SH6 via Cromwell (115 km, ~1.5 hrs) is the safer winter alternative. If Crown Range looks clear, take it — it’s exceptional.

📷 Crown Range summit (if taking that route): Panoramic view down over the Cardrona Valley — one of the great NZ road trip shots. 5-minute stop.

Beginner slopes, ski school for both kids (age 3+). Hire gear at the base. Warm waterproof layers + sunscreen — UV is strong on snow. Allow the full morning. A half-day ski lesson for the kids is a great option.
Coronet Peak base lodge café — Hot food and warming drinks on the mountain.

Recommendation: Option A. You’ve already done a petting zoo on Day 2 and a farm tour on Day 6, so Willowbank offers something genuinely different — native NZ wildlife like kiwi, tuatara, and weta that you simply can’t see elsewhere. A cold June weekday in Christchurch city with tired kids at the end of a big trip isn’t the best trade-off. Tekapo Springs in the morning keeps the holiday vibe going, and Willowbank makes for a memorable, low-key final activity before a relaxed last night at the Novotel.
